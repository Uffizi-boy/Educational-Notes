Noise Pollution

Presence of excessive or unwanted sounds in the environment is called noise pollution.

=> Major causes of noise pollution are :-
  • Sounds of vehicles.
  • Sounds of explosions including bursting of crackers.
  • Sounds of machines.
  • Sounds of loudspeakers.
  • Sounds of electronic equipments.
=> Harms of noise pollution are :-
  • Lack of sleep.
  • Hypertension (high blood pressure).
  • Anxiety.
  • Temporary or even permanent impairment of hearing.
  • Tinnitus
  • Insomnia.
  • Cardiovascular diseases.
  • Mental health issues like stress and dipression.
  • Cognitive impairment.
  • Speech interference.
=> Ways to reduce noise pollution are :-
  • Silencing devices must be installed in aircraft engines, transport vehicles, industrial machines, and home appliances.
  • All noisy operations must be conducted away from residential areas.
  • Noise producing industries should be set up away from residential areas.
  • Use of automobile horns shoul be minimised.
  • T.V. and music systems should be run at low volumes.
  • Trees must be planted along the roads and around building to cut down on the sounds reaching the residents.
=> some Facts about noise pollution:-
  • 12,000 people prematurely died in India each year Due to noise pollution
  • around 6.3% of the population in world is affected by hearing impairment and related diseases due to noise pollution.
  • Noise pollution impacts millions of people on a daily basis.
  • United States of America, Italy and China has the most noise pollution.
  • Zurich, Switzerland is considered to have the least noise pollution in the world:
